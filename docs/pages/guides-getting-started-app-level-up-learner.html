Logging On

Assumptions;
  • You are a member of the learning community using selfdriven.
  • You have been emailed a username and password by your learning community facilitator.
1
Go to levelup.slfdrvn.app .
Always check that you are on this website.
We suggest bookmarking it in your browser.
2
Enter the username and password sent to you and click Log On.
If you have forgotten your password then click Forgot you password?.
3
You are now logged on!

Securing Your User Account

1
The first thing to do is protect your user account using 2FA.
We do this using One Time Passwords i.e. the 6 digit codes that change every 30 seconds.
If already have Google Authenticator, Authy or an equivalent installed on your phone then you are all good.
If not sure which one to install, then we suggest installing Authy on your phone.
Once set up, you can move to step 2!
2
Now we need to enable 2FA for your user account.
In the left hand menu click on My Profile.
Then scroll down and click Set up or reset 2FA.
Use your 2FA app (e.g. Authy) to scan the QR code and follow the instuctions.
3
Your account is now better secured.
All the usual "Don't share your username/password" advice applies!
If you trust the device you are logging on to, you can click Trust this Browser, and then you won't have to enter the 2FA code everytime you log on.

Editing Your Profile

1
You can manage parts of your profile.
In the left hand menu click on My Profile.
Click Edit Profile.
2
As a start we suggest editing the following details:
  • About Me
  • Greatest Strength
  • Upload a Profile Image
    Be sensible, and if you need help with what that means, see the "What is Appropriate" rules.

Setting Up Your Level Up Profile

1
In the left hand menu click Dashboard
Then click the Set Up Your Level Up Profile button.
2
Now you can go through each of the sections and enter the information for:
  • I want to Improve
  • By Doing Challenges
  • With
  • Supported By
  • For
3
If you need help with what skills you think you should improve or ideas for challenges then we suggest:
  • Speak with fellow learners.
  • Speak with fellow learnering-partners.
  • On the By Doing Challenges tab click Help, I Need Inspiration
  • If you have access to a Generative AI agent, e.g. like ChatGPT, then ask it for ideas.
4
Once you have set up your Level Up profile and discussed with your learning-partners you can then move onto starting a challenge project.
The Challenge project helps you manage tasks, attach files, record reflections etc as you work through it.

Creating a Challenge Project

1
In the left hand menu click Projects
Then click on the Challenge Projects tab.
Based on your Level Up profile, will see an information box with note on the challenges you have not yet created projects for.
Click Show to see the By Doing Challenges from your Level Up profile.
Click Create.
Then click Create as Project.
You will then see the challenge project just created, ready for to start working on it.

Updating Your Challenge Project

1
In the left hand menu click Projects
Then click the challenge you are working on.
Then click on the Challenge Projects tab.
Then click on the project you want to work on.
2
To start working on the challenge, click on the To Dos (Tasks) tab
  • Click on the To Do you are up to.
  • Or click Add To Do Task to track progress, attached photos & documents etc

Attaching a File & Linking to a URL

1
Attaching a file to a project task:
  • Click the cloud up arrow.
  • Select the file(s) e.g. photos of your work.
    If you are using a mobile phone you can also choose to take a photo.
  • It will automatically upload
2
Linking a webpage (e.g. Google Doc, Slides etc) to a project task:
  • Click the chain-link icon.
  • Paste in the URL.
  • Click Save.

What's Up?

1
Click on What's Up to see a feed of updates from your learning partners.

Completing a Challenge

1
Once all the the tasks are 100% complete you can set a project as complete.
Your learning partners can then assign final reflections and assign skills etc
If the project supports it, you will also be assign selfdriven Community Tokens (SDC) that can be used within your learning community or beyond by moving them "on-chain".

Viewing Connections

1
In the left hand menu click Connections
You will then see a list of your learning-partner connections.
Click on the down arrow to see endorsements, reflections on you and skills them have assigned to you.

Viewing your Achievements & Skills

1
When you complete a project your learning-partner will assign you achievements with associated skills based on the level you acheived; Aware, Capable, Knowledgeable, Natural.
You can view achievements & skills at:
  • Achievements.
  • My Profile.
selfdriven Universal Skills Set Explorer
2
You can download a summary of your challenge project for yourself or sharing:
For a summary:
  • Challenges.
  • Open the challenge project
  • Click Project Story
  • Click the File With Down Arrow button.
For your all of learning summary;
  • My Profile.
  • Click the File With Down Arrow button.

Viewing Your Tokens

1
Click Tokens
You can then use your tokens in a number of ways, including moving them to your "on-chain" wallet.
They can also be used when working out your next learning steps.

What is Appropriate

1
Generally be caring, be kind.
2
When upating your profile "About Me", or any information that can be viewed by others;
Do not include anything that would cause harm to you or anyone else.
It doesn't add any value to you, or meet our aim of creating a least-harmful society.
3
When upating your profile image, it can't contain anything that would cause harm to others and no nudity.
4
It's covered in items 1 & 2, but to be clear don't share anything that is obvisouly not intend to be shared.
i.e. information relating to a project that you are working on in a team.
5
If you do want to share information, check with the other people that also share ownership of it.
